My notes

- The [Henry Adams curve](https://wimflyc.blogspot.com/2021/01/the-henry-adams-curve-closer-look.html) marks the sudden drop-off in energy production, which coincides with the great
 American stagnation. Energy production is critical for technological advancements because of 
 [Jevons paradox](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jevons_paradox); as technology advances it generally becomes more energy efficient, which
 increases demand in turn meaning higher energy consumption (think AI data centres and robotics).

- Cold fusion wasn’t always just pure hype; federal research agendas moved away from this failed experiment
- The optimal architecture for flying cars must account for the maximal energy expenditure during takeoff and landing i.e., last energy density. VTOLs are the superior form factor.
- The environmental movement is the modern day equivalent to the Baptist and bootleggers. Climate change has been overdetermined and reducing our energy consumption will have more negative effect then increasing sea level temperatures by 2C. We cannot slow down energy production—only develop more sustainable methods.
- Fusion theoretically unlocks energy to cheap to metre however we’re very far. DT fusion energy at 100mK unleashes 80% neutrons; which cannot efficiently harness directly into electricity and tritium is a very expensive radioactive prime material.
    - D-D fusion uses a non-radioactive abundant prime material found in deep waters and only emits 35% of energy as neutrons at ~400mK. 100mK is already a challenge, 4x that beyond currently superconducting technology (assuming a magnetic confinemnet design).
- Even fission involves a complicated supply chain. Cf-251 is essential for kick-starting the fission reactor, yet it's produced only in microgram quantities ([MIT](https://learn.mit.edu/video-playlist/detail/7715?playlist=7710)).
- Flying cars will be an ultimate unlock. Cheaper than drilling tunnels and towards a 3D transportation system that can one day support a planet Earth of 100b people, and a galactic species of many hundreds of billions of humans.
- Chemical rockets are currently the cheapest way to escape Earth’s gravitational field. What about gigastructures that tower of the troposphere with giant elevators? This is possible with abundant materials and atomic energy.
- Kardashev’s scale is a great way to measure progress relative to the rest of the universe

<span slot="summary">Incognito: The Secret Lives of the Brain</span>

Most memorable line:

> If our brains were simple enough to be understood, we would not be smart enough to understand them.

My notes

- Optical nerves are unidirectional and there are 10x more nerves going from your brain to yours eyes then the other way around i.e., we see with our brains!
- Our eye has a blind spot which is undetectable unless physically tested. The entire time our brains are filling in the blind spot based on the surrounding pixels.

<Image
  src={eyeTest}
  class="w-full max-w-xs mx-auto"

  thumbhash="996c116f16806a9687878778788887777887087888908707"
  alt="Two-panel blind spot demo: green panel with a centered plus sign and yellow panel with a vertical line and dot—used to reveal the visual blind spot"
  caption="Blind spot test for your eyes."

- 1/3 of cognition in our brains is dedicated to vision alone
- Decouple the concept of seeing with vision. Even blind humans have vision, since the other 2/3 of the brain (which cannot see) increases in processing power. As an experiment a blind person attached a camera to his head, hooked up to a grid of electrodes on their tongue which signalled objects detected straight ahead from the camera to climb Mount Everest. Blind people also developed excellent hearing, some can use echo location to navigate.
- Neuroplasticity is a measure of the brains ability to attenuate new cognition and it **does not decrease with age**, but with practice! Always keep your brain working and learning.
