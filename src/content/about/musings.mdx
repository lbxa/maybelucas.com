# Musings

<Toggle>
<span slot="summary">My super philosophy</span>

Scale, abstraction and curiosity are the essential primitives for understanding anything.

_Abstraction_ is the framework that allows us to zoom in and out of different _scales_.

Quarks and leptons, when combined, form the atomic scaffolding of organic matter. Organic molecules, when combined, form the cellular scaffolding for bacteria, plants, animals, and ultimately, conscious systems capable of reflecting on the structure that made them.

Groking large scales down to the primitives requires traversal through long and dense abstraction chains - something only possible with relentless _curiosity_.
</Toggle>

<Toggle>
<span slot="summary">_How_ problems are easier than _what_ problems</span>

*“What are we going to build?”* versus *“How are we going to build it?”*

This might read as a misnomer since it's difficult to arrive at *how* without deciding on something to work on - the *what*.

The ideal problem is one where the *how* is more difficult to solve than the *what*. Ruling out the edge cases where both are either trivial or intractable.

*How* problems are those where the *what* is easy to understand. For example, cryosleep chambers, anti-matter propulsion engines and nuclear fusion. Their impact on society is non-trivial but how do we do it?

Contrast this with the quintessential *what* problem: Blockchain. The how has been well understood for years now, distributed consensus, cryptographic verification, programmable ledgers. But what should this actually be used for, where it’s better than conventional systems?
</Toggle>

<Toggle>
<span slot="summary">What happened to grandeur?</span>

Where did the large marble columns and painted ceilings go? Where can we seek modern grandeur?

Greco-futurism is an inspiring style. It invokes awe.

Brutalism, despite its melancholic undertone, still invokes permanence. A durable structure that will outlast you, no matter your opinion towards it.

Today’s buildings, don’t look like they'd stand a chance against Godzilla.
</Toggle>

<Toggle>
<span slot="summary">Have a bias towards action</span>

When it doubt act. Action produces information. New information is something we can act on, so on and so forth.

A moving man will meet his luck.
</Toggle>

<Toggle>
<span slot="summary">Human-computer interfaces</span>

Carefully crafted human-computer interaction is vital to fusing the efficiency of machines with the creativity of humans.

GUIs are one way to do this today and we should strive to them simple, [not simpler](https://www.quora.com/What-exactly-did-Einstein-mean-by-Everything-should-be-made-as-simple-as-possible-but-not-simpler). 

Tools are not designed to replace us, they're designed to enhance us.

Robotics is the ultimate form factor. Physical technology built to help humans interact with the real world.
</Toggle>

<span slot="summary">The case for full-stack+</span>

In the context of internet-based product and services, if full-stack refers to frontend + backend, then full-stack+ includes data science and machine learning.

In hardware, it has been widely accepted that to provide the best possible user experience, you have to vertically
integrate. It should be no different with pure software products.
